What should our lesson plans include?

A successful lesson plan typically integrates three key components: learning objectives, learning activities, and assessment strategies. 

  • Learning Objectives: Define exactly what students should know or be able to do by the end of the lesson. Use the SMART criteria—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound—to ensure goals are focused and clear.
  • The Procedure (Step-by-Step Delivery):
  • Introduction/Hook: A brief section (5–10 minutes) to grab students' attention using a story, video, provocative question, or real-world example.
  • Direct Instruction: Explicit teaching of new content through lectures, modeling, or demonstrations.
  • Guided Practice: Opportunities for students to practice new skills with teacher support, often in pairs or small groups.
  • Independent Practice: Tasks for students to complete on their own to demonstrate mastery.
  • Assessments:
  • Formative: Ongoing checks for understanding during the lesson (e.g., polls, "fist-to-five" signals, or brief quizzes).
  • Summative: Evaluation at the end of the lesson or unit (e.g., an exit ticket, short written reflection, or project) to see if objectives were met.
  • Closure/Wrap-up: A summary to reinforce key points, tie the lesson to future topics, and clarify any misunderstandings.
  • Teacher Reflection: Notes made after the lesson about what worked well, what didn't, and what should be changed for next time.
